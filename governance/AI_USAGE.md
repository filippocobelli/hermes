## Purpose

This document defines how artificial intelligence tools are used within the HERMES project,
and the boundaries of their role.

## Policy

This project makes use of AI-assisted drafting tools for documentation, software architecture
discussions and code generation support.

Artificial intelligence is used as a technical assistant, not as a scientific authority.

All scientific decisions, methodological choices, validation procedures and project governance
remain under human responsibility.

## Scope of AI Assistance

AI assistance may be used for:

- drafting and editing documentation;
- discussing software architecture options;
- generating code scaffolding and boilerplate;
- reviewing methodology for bias, confounding variables, and logical consistency
  (Scientific Method Reviewer role).

AI assistance shall **not** be used as:

- the final authority on any scientific conclusion;
- a substitute for human review of methodology, data, or results;
- a source cited as scientific evidence.

## Disclosure Requirement

Every document or dataset produced with meaningful AI assistance shall note this in its
Change Log or Reviewers field, per the Documentation Standard (HERMES-FND-000).

## Human Responsibility

The project Owner (or designated human reviewers) retain full responsibility for:

- final approval of methodology;
- validation of results;
- any published conclusion;
- project governance decisions.
